Braised Pork Belly Rice

The humble braised pork belly rice, also known as 滷肉饭, has been a comfort dish of mine for many years. What was once thought to be a difficult dish to master has become a common dish for me to cook at home. I learnt to cook this dish when I was studying abroad in the UK and shared it with my flatmates who absolutely loved it as well! Without further ado, let's get cracking with the recipe!

Ingredients

  • 2 small red onions
  • 5 cloves of garlic
  • 1 mushroom of choice (optional)
  • 4 1cm-thick slices of pork belly
  • 70ml light soy sauce
  • 70-100ml Shaoxing wine (or any sort of cooking wine available)
  • 50g brown sugar
  • 100-200ml water

Steps

  1. Chop the red onions, garlic, mushrooms, and pork belly into small pieces.
  2. Add the pork belly to your pot, frying it off on medium heat for about 3 minutes.
  3. Add in the onions, followed by the mushrooms and garlic, sauteeing it for another 5 minutes.
  4. Add in about 3 tablespoons of light soy sauce to the pot to scrape the pork belly from the bottom of the pot, followed by the Shaoxing wine.
  5. Add in the rest of the light soy sauce followed by the brown sugar.
  6. Pour in approximately 100-200ml water and cover the pot, turning the heat down to a low simmer.

Tips

  • The longer you leave the pork to braise, the more tender and flavourful it will be.
  • Keep an eye out to ensure the water does not boil off before the meat is done braising!
  • This dish is best served with rice.
  • As an optional step, you may add in a boiled egg while the meat is braising for a soy sauce braised egg as well.
